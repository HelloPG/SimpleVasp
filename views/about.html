Overview

ValuationCompass is a tool for individual investors. Individual investors often have difficulty in estimating equity prices/values. ValuationCompass makes it easy to prepare your own valuation spreadsheet. You can compare your spreadsheet with 'Crowd' spreadsheet which is average of estimates by all the other users.

A GPS gives the exact co-ordinates as opposed to a Compass which provides approximate location awareness. Hence the name - ValuationCompass. The tool does not claim to provide exact value of stocks.

ValuationCompass is not a substitute to forecasts by equity analysts. Valuation analysis that equity analysts do is much more involved than ValuationCompass and takes into account many more variables. ValuationCompass' goal is to provide a context within which company valuations exist.

Methodology

FCFE (Future Cash Flows to Equity): Measure of how much cash can be paid to the equity shareholders of the company after all expenses and reinvestment are paid.
CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model): Provides risk adjusted, expected rate of return for a stock. This rate of return is considered as Cost of Equity(COE). It is used to discount future cash flows and calculate present value of sum of all those cash flows.

ValuationCompass uses cash flows for only next 20 years because when companies buy or sell other companies, they typically use profilt multiplier of 20 or less. In reality, a Utility company may have longer, more stable lifespan than that of a Technology company.

User estimates cash flows for next 5 years. After that for the next 15 years, yearly cash flow is assumed to be same and equal to 5th year cash flow. In reality, cash flows will either increase or decrease but it is impossible to forecast those changes. Sum of discounted cash flows for last 15 years is calulated using 'Present Value of Annuity' formula where annuity is equal to 5th year cash flow.

Present Value (in year 6) of cash flows for next 15 years = FCF5×[1-(1+COE)-15]÷COE

Sum of all the discounted cash flows (for 5+15 years) = Σ( FCFn/(1+COE)n )

Intrinsic Value of Stock = Sum of all the discounted cash flows / Number Of Shares
